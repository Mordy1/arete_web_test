General questions

How to Incorporate a New Company?

There are 3 ways to incorporate a new company:
1. Direct incorporation, where you can register your company directly to SSM.
2. Name reservation, where you reserve a proposed name with SSM before registration.
3. Via A Company platform, where the process will be hassle-free. Simply sign up and get started on the platform; we will take care of the rest.

What are the Key Requirements to Incorporate a New Private Limited Company (Sdn Bhd)?

To incorporate or register a new private limited company (Sdn. Bhd.) in Malaysia, the company must have:
- 1 or more directors, who must be 18 years of age or older.
- At least 1 director residing in Malaysia.
- Minimum 1 and maximum 50 shareholders. (Can be Malaysian citizen, non-Malaysian citizen, or a corporate body)
- Minimum paid-up capital of RM1000
- A registered address in Malaysia.

Who qualifies to be a director of a Sdn. Bhd. company in Malaysia?

To be a director of a private limited company (Sdn. Bhd.) in Malaysia, you must:
- Be at least 18 years old,
- Reside in Malaysia
- Not an undischarged bankrupt
- Have not been disqualified by the Court
- Have not been convicted of an offense involving bribery, fraud, or dishonesty.

How long does it take to incorporate or register a Sdn. Bhd. in Malaysia?

After submitting all related information to SSM, the whole process usually takes 1-3 working days for the process, provided there is no query from SSM and is subject to the approval of the SSM officer in charge.

What name can I use for my company?

You may refer to the full guidelines from SSM before naming your company.

Can a foreigner set up a Sdn. Bhd. company in Malaysia?

Yes, provided the individual is over 18 years old. Additionally, the individual named as the director of the company must reside in Malaysia—either as principal or only place of residence.

Can I incorporate a company with 1 Director and 1 Shareholder?

Yes, that is possible. However, the sole director must reside in Malaysia.

What should I do after my company is incorporated?

Within 30 days of the incorporation date, the company/you must:
1. Appoint the first company secretary.
2. Lodge the constitution with SSM; a fee of RM200 is chargeable. Failure to do so is an offense.
3. Open company bank account.
4. Register with LHDN, KWSP, Customs and/or other government bodies or agencies, whichever is applicable.
